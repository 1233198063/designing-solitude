## 起点 / The Starting Point

<!-- 用自己的话写：是什么时刻让你决定开始写关于独处的文章？ -->
<!-- In your own words: what moment made you decide to write about solitude? -->

在大量使用AI之后，我担心属于我自己的表达被取代被淹没。于是我在今年年初决定要重拾表达，我的方式是在小红书记录我的想法，配上我自己拍摄的图片作为封面。

但我又是一个非常注重隐私的人，如果我的文章不够具体，全是总结式的语言的话，我自己都不爱看。

恰好我有了大量的独处时间，在对年龄，人生，职业的种种迷茫和焦虑中，我选择阅读，听音乐，散步，写作，书法等等来充实我的生活。我发现，当我认真生活，我的心又落回了实处，焦虑和迷茫也就渐渐淡去了。

与此同时，我也有了很多新的感悟和体会能书写出来和大家分享，也就是在独处中，我做了什么，想了什么，感受到了什么......都可以作为文章的主题来分享，这就是我开始写独处文章的原因。它不是一个突然冒出来的想法，而是在我这个生命阶段，自然而然出现的我想要做的事。

After using AI extensively, I started to worry that my own voice might gradually be replaced or drowned out. At the beginning of this year, I decided that I wanted to reclaim my own way of expressing myself. I chose to do that by writing on Xiaohongshu and pairing each post with a photo that I took myself.

At the same time, I'm also a very private person. If my writing is too vague or full of abstract conclusions, I don't even enjoy reading it myself. I wanted my writing to come from real experiences instead.

Around the same time, I found myself with a lot of time alone. While feeling uncertain and anxious about my age, my future, and my career, I filled my days with reading, listening to music, taking walks, writing, practicing calligraphy, and many other quiet activities.

Slowly, I realized that whenever I was fully present in my own life, my mind became calmer. The anxiety and uncertainty gradually faded, and in their place came new thoughts, observations, and experiences that I genuinely wanted to share.

That's how this series began. Every piece is simply about what I did, what I thought, and what I felt during moments of solitude. Those ordinary moments became the starting point for my writing.

Writing about solitude wasn't a sudden idea. It was something that emerged naturally during this stage of my life.

## 这件事对我意味着什么 / What This Means to Me

<!-- 不是"我想要影响力"或"我想建立品牌"，而是更私人的动机。
     比如：独处给了我什么？为什么值得写下来分享？-->
<!-- Not "I want influence" or "I want a brand" — something more personal.
     For example: what has solitude given me? Why is it worth writing about and sharing? -->

直接照搬我在小红书写的第一篇有关独处的文章：“独自去做一些事情的时候，更能让我屏蔽外界的因素去发现自己能做到什么，真正喜欢什么，情绪是从哪儿来的……
	
我开始更清楚自己的边界，不再那么容易因为别人的评价怀疑自己，也不再急着去迎合外界的标准。很奇妙的是，越了解自己，也越能理解别人。因为知道每个人都有各自的成长速度和各自的边界，所以会多一些宽容，也更愿意给彼此留出空间和自由。

独处时能认识自己也能探索世界。”

When I do things on my own, it's much easier to tune out outside expectations and discover what I'm actually capable of, what I truly enjoy, and where my emotions come from.

As I spend more time alone, I've become much clearer about my own boundaries. I'm no longer so quick to doubt myself because of other people's opinions, and I don't feel the same need to live up to someone else's definition of success.

What's interesting is that the more I understand myself, the more I understand other people. I realize that everyone grows at their own pace and has their own boundaries. That has made me more patient, more accepting, and more willing to give both myself and others the space and freedom we need.

Solitude has become a way for me to explore both myself and the world.

## 我想给读者留下什么 / What I Want Readers to Take Away

<!-- 读者读完我的文章后，我希望他们感受到什么？带走什么？ -->
<!-- After reading my articles, what do I hope readers feel? What do I hope they carry with them? -->
希望害怕孤独的人能够开始享受独处的乐趣，希望能给大家带来一些新的视角和力量，希望读者能带走一些乐观有趣的生活方式。勇气很重要，希望我的勇气能够传递出去！

I hope people who are afraid of being alone can slowly discover that solitude can be joyful.

I hope my writing offers a different perspective, a little encouragement, and reminds people that there are many beautiful ways to live a life.

Most of all, I hope my courage can be passed on to someone else. I hope it encourages people to try something on their own, to trust themselves a little more, and to discover a version of themselves they haven't met yet.
